Website Evaluations

Apple

The Apple website is a superb example of effective design—it features a clean layout, intuitive navigation, and a clear visual hierarchy that speaks directly to their brand identity.

  • Contrast: Uses a mostly light background with dark text and strategically highlighted elements to drive focus and readability.
  • Repetition: Consistently employs the same fonts, iconography, and color schemes throughout the site, reinforcing brand consistency.
  • Alignment: All elements are carefully aligned, giving the layout a balanced appearance that guides the user's eyes smoothly.
  • Proximity: Related information is grouped in a logical manner, which makes browsing both effortless and engaging.

Angelfire

Angelfire hosts many sites that reflect an outdated aesthetic where design choices often feel cluttered and disorganized. This makes it a prime example to discuss what happens when effective design principles aren’t followed.

  • Contrast: Often suffers from poor color contrast, with text blending into backgrounds and making content difficult to read.
  • Repetition: There is little consistency in fonts, colors, or design elements, contributing to a fragmented visual experience.
  • Alignment: Many pages display misaligned elements that contribute to a chaotic, hard-to-follow layout.
  • Proximity: Content and design elements are haphazardly grouped together, leading to cluttered pages where the focus is lost.
